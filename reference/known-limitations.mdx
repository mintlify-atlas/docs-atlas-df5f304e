---
title: 'Known limitations'
description: 'Hardware and software limitations of nRFBox, including power constraints, radio range, BLE compatibility, and GPIO conflicts.'
---

<AccordionGroup>

  <Accordion title="Power limitations">

    <Warning>
      Running all three NRF24 modules simultaneously at `RF24_PA_MAX` can exceed the onboard power regulator's capacity.

      **Symptoms:** intermittent failures, module disconnects, or device resets.

      **Recommendation:** use a stable 3.3V regulator with at least 500mA capacity and add decoupling capacitors (100µF + 100nF) near each NRF24 VCC/GND pair.
    </Warning>

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Jamming range">

    <Note>
      Effective jamming range is short — typically 1–5 meters depending on the environment.

      - Signal strength weakens with distance; reliable disruption beyond ~10 meters is unlikely.
      - High-interference environments (crowded 2.4GHz spectrum) further reduce effectiveness.
    </Note>

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="BLE Spoofer reliability">

    <Note>
      BLE Spoofer is rated **Low** reliability. Not all target devices respond to spoofed BLE advertisements.

      - Compatibility varies by iOS/Android version and device model.
      - Best used in controlled testing scenarios rather than general deployment.
    </Note>

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Sour Apple attack">

    <Note>
      The Sour Apple attack's effectiveness depends on the iOS version and device settings.

      - Newer iOS versions (17+) include partial mitigations against BLE advertisement flooding.
      - The attack requires close physical proximity to target devices.
    </Note>

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Scanner and Analyzer missed detections">

    <Note>
      In high-interference 2.4GHz environments, fast-switching signals may not be captured.

      - The NRF24L01 RPD (Received Power Detector) register only detects signals above **-64 dBm**.
      - Signals below this threshold are invisible to the scanner and analyzer.
    </Note>

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="NRF24 Radio A and SD card GPIO 5 conflict">

    <Note>
      Radio A CE pin and the SD card CS pin both use **GPIO 5**.

      - Radio A cannot be active at the same time as SD card access.
      - The firmware handles this automatically: SD access only occurs during the "Update Firmware" option in the Settings menu, which powers down all three radios first via `setRadiosNeutralState()`.
      - Under normal operation this conflict is not exposed to the user.
    </Note>

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="BLE and WiFi mutual exclusion">

    <Note>
      The ESP32's BLE and WiFi stacks share the same underlying radio hardware and cannot run concurrently.

      - Modes that use BLE (BLE Jammer, BLE Spoofer, Sour Apple, BLE Scan) call `esp_wifi_stop()` on startup.
      - Modes that use WiFi (WiFi Scan, Deauther) cannot simultaneously run BLE functions.
      - Switching between BLE and WiFi modes requires a mode change through the main menu, which reinitialises the appropriate stack.
    </Note>

  </Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>
